We are a research team based at the [School of Energy and Environment](https://www.cityu.edu.hk/see/) and [State Key Laboratory of Marine Environental Health](https://www.cityu.edu.hk/sklmeh/), City University of Hong Kong. The Hydro-Env Modelling Group at CityUHK specializes in investigating the complex dynamics of aquatic environments. Our research core lies in the integration of process-based physical modeling and data-driven computational approaches to understand and predict the transport and fate of microbial and chemical contaminants. By bridging the gap between theoretical modeling and field-scale applications, we strive to provide robust scientific insights for sustainable water quality management and ecosystem resilience.

Key Research Interests
======
**Hydro-Environmental & Ecological Modelling**
We specialize in developing and applying advanced hydrodynamic, water quality, and ecological models. By integrating process-based mechanistic simulations with data-driven techniques, we significantly enhance the predictive accuracy and robustness of models across complex aquatic systems under diverse environmental conditions.

**Transport & Fate of Contaminants**
We investigate the environmental behavior of microbial and chemical contaminants across watershed-to-coastal continua. Our research elucidates the mechanisms of pollutant transport, transformation, and ultimate fate, providing a rigorous scientific foundation for regional ecological risk assessment and mitigation.

**Pioneering Digital Waters**
We are advancing the paradigm of Digital Waters by creating high-fidelity, data-integrated replicas of aquatic systems. By fusing real-time sensing (e.g., buoy networks) with predictive modelling, we provide a dynamic digital environment for stress-testing scenarios, optimizing management strategies, and visualizing ecological shifts in real-time. This technological evolution empowers a transition from reactive monitoring to proactive, intelligent water governance.

**Multi-source Big Data & Data Mining**
Leveraging heterogeneous environmental datasets, we employ advanced data mining and machine learning to extract critical spatiotemporal patterns. By identifying non-linear relationships between environmental stressors and ecological responses, we provide new paradigms for the analysis of complex aquatic systems.

**Remote Sensing & Hydro-Env Modelling Synergy**
We bridge macro-scale remote sensing with high-fidelity numerical models to bridge the gap between observation and prediction. By developing advanced parameter inversion algorithms, we integrate satellite, UAV, and ground-based data directly into our hydrodynamic-ecological simulations. This "Observation-to-Prediction" pipeline enables comprehensive monitoring and high-precision early warning for complex aquatic environments.
